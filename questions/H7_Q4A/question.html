Design a CPRE 281 Style Falling Edge Detector


Design a circuit like from CPRE 281 to place in the "Falling Edge Detector" box of the UART Interrupt subsystem below. This will be used to detect when a new UART frame begins to arrive. Recall the start symbol of a UART frame is indicated by the UART RX wire transitioning from high to low (i.e. a falling or negative edge occurs). To keep your circuit simple, use the following assumptions:

  • Your circuit should pulse the “detect” signal for 1 clock cycle whenever a falling edge on the RX wire occurs. In other words, “detect” should become '1' for exactly one clock cycle.
  • You are limited to using the following types of components:
    • AND
    • OR
    • NOT
    • XOR
    • D Flip-Flop
  • Assume any time the RX wire goes low, it will stay low for at least one clock cycle.
  • Inputs:
    • UART RX wire
    • Clock signal
  • Outputs:
    • Detect signal
